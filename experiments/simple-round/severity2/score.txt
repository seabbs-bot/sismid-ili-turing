simple-round / severity2 (SEASON-SEVERITY family, round-2 iteration on
the round-1 winner)
Scored on VALIDATION seasons only (1, 2), natural scale, per
docs/contracts.md experimental integrity.

Starting point: `season/generate.jl` (climatology + backfill,
MODEL_ID "seabbs_bot-season"), 0.3004 mean validation WIS / 0.2781 on
the full simple-round leaderboard -- the round-1 winner. This family
tests one addition on top of that model, motivated by
docs/eda/04-cross-location.md's "shared season-severity effect"
(off-diagonal seasonal-amplitude correlation mean 0.68 across the 11
locations -- a whole season tends to run uniformly severe or mild, not
independently per location): estimate how severe the CURRENT season
is running, from its trajectory so far (observed level vs. that
location's own climatology, over the in-season weeks already seen up
to the forecast origin), POOL that estimate across all 11 locations,
and scale the climatology term's contribution to the FORWARD forecast
by the pooled factor. The fitted AR(6)+climatology regression itself
is untouched; only the future climatology values fed into the forward
simulation are adjusted, via a shrinkage parameter `LAMBDA_SEVERITY`
(0 = no adjustment, exactly reproduces climatology-backfill; 1 = full
raw pooled estimate).

baseline (climatology + backfill, LAMBDA_SEVERITY=0): 0.3004 (sd 0.389)

Every one of these 16 combinations (2 forms x 4 lambdas x 2 pooling
stats) is WORSE than the lambda=0 baseline (0.3004), and within each
(form, pooling) pair, mean_wis increases MONOTONICALLY with lambda --
more severity adjustment is uniformly worse, not just on average at
one setting. sd_wis falls as lambda rises (the adjustment does make
forecasts more consistent across tasks), but that is bought entirely
at the cost of mean accuracy, the wrong trade for a scoring rule that
selects on mean WIS.

(multiplicative, mean pooling, whole-season-to-date mean estimate)

lambda=0.05 -> mean_wis=0.3005
lambda=0.10 -> mean_wis=0.3005
lambda=0.15 -> mean_wis=0.3006
lambda=0.20 -> mean_wis=0.3008

Confirms the degradation starts immediately at lambda > 0 -- there is
no small positive lambda that beats the baseline; lambda=0 is the
optimum of a monotonically-increasing curve, not a local minimum
missed by the coarser grid above.

recent=6 weeks: lambda=0.25 -> 0.3021 | 0.50 -> 0.3099 |
                0.75 -> 0.3236 | 1.00 -> 0.3428
recent=4 weeks: lambda=0.25 -> 0.3023 | 0.50 -> 0.3116 |
                0.75 -> 0.3277 | 1.00 -> 0.3503

Narrower recency windows are WORSE at every matched lambda than the
whole-season-to-date estimate above (e.g. lambda=1.0: 0.343-0.350 vs.
0.308) -- fewer weeks of support makes the pooled estimate noisier,
not more locally relevant, and the degradation is steeper.

=== Sweep 4: median-centered per-location estimate ===
(multiplicative, median pooling; per-location estimate is the MEDIAN

lambda=0.25 -> 0.3012 | 0.50 -> 0.3024 | 0.75 -> 0.3043 | 1.00 -> 0.3067

Same story: monotonically worse than the lambda=0 baseline, though
marginally less bad than the mean-centered version at matched lambda
-- median-centering does not rescue the approach, just softens the
degradation slightly.

CONCLUSION: shared season-severity scaling does NOT help here. Across
every axis varied -- form (multiplicative/additive), cross-location
pooling statistic (mean/median), per-location estimate centering
(mean/median), and estimation window (whole season-to-date / last 6
weeks / last 4 weeks) -- adding any nonzero amount of severity scaling
on top of climatology-backfill made validation WIS worse,
monotonically in the adjustment strength, with no small-lambda sweet
spot (the fine scan near 0 confirms lambda=0 is a true minimum, not an
artifact of a coarse grid). The likely mechanism: by the time enough
in-season weeks exist to estimate a severity factor at all
(MIN_SEVERITY_WEEKS=3), the AR(6) lags already see the current
season's actual recent values directly and have adapted to it running
hot or cold; the pooled severity term mostly duplicates information
the AR component already has, and what it adds beyond that is
estimation noise -- worse, not better, with narrower/more "local"
recency windows, which is the opposite of what a genuine signal would
show.

This is a clean negative result, not a tuning failure to iterate
further on: the cross-location amplitude correlation from
docs/eda/04-cross-location.md is real (mean r=0.68), but the AR(6)+
climatology model already captures most of what that correlation would
add, because its lagged terms see each location's own current-season
trajectory directly every forecast origin.

BEST: LAMBDA_SEVERITY = 0.0 (severity scaling disabled), i.e.
identical to plain climatology + backfill (`season/generate.jl`,
"seabbs_bot-season"). experiments/simple-round/severity2/generate.jl
ships the full severity apparatus (for reproducibility of this
negative result -- flip LAMBDA_SEVERITY above 0 to see the
degradation directly) but with it set to its empirically-best value of
0. Validated end-to-end: `julia --project=<repo> generate.jl`
reproduces mean_wis=0.3004, sd_wis=0.389, n_tasks=2596 exactly, byte-
for-byte the same model as `seabbs_bot-season`.
