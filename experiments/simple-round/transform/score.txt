=== Headline finding: LOG beats FOURTH-ROOT, clearly ===

fourthroot is the EDA's flattest-variance pick
(docs/eda/01-series-overview.md), but WIS is scored on the NATURAL
percentage scale, and there `log` wins by ~4% mean WIS (0.3537 vs
0.3684) and is also more stable (sd_wis 0.419 vs 0.471). Variance-
stabilisation on a log-variance regression and WIS-minimisation on the
natural scale are different objectives: fourth-root's flatter modelling
-scale residuals do not translate into a better-calibrated natural-
scale predictive distribution once the AR(6) + Gaussian-innovation
back-transform is applied. Every row between log (p=0) and fourthroot
(p=0.25) sits monotonically between them on WIS (log1p, then a small
gap, then fourthroot's neighbourhood), so this is not just one lucky
transform -- lower power (closer to log) is better across the board for
this scoring objective, except at the identity/sqrt end where WIS gets
much worse again. log1p (0.3771) is worse than plain log (0.3537)
despite cutting less far than log/EPS: consistent with
docs/eda/01-series-overview.md's own finding that log1p under-
stabilises variance in this data (its "+1" shift matters at these
small percentage values), and evidently that shows up in WIS too, not
just in the variance-stabilisation diagnostic.

=== Per-location optimal transform: real effect, but numerically
     dangerous applied naively ===

Per-location fitted Box-Cox power (grid MLE, -0.6:0.02:1.0, on
season_year <= 2016 finalized data, matching the exponent convention
`w -> w^p`):

  US National   -0.34      HHS Region 6   -0.14
  HHS Region 1   0.00      HHS Region 7    0.04
  HHS Region 2   0.12      HHS Region 8    0.16
  HHS Region 3  -0.18      HHS Region 9    0.24   <- highest
  HHS Region 4  -0.12      HHS Region 10   0.00
  HHS Region 5  -0.30 (lowest)

This confirms docs/eda/01-series-overview.md and
docs/eda/07-region9-deepdive.md: Region 9 is the one location that
wants a materially HIGHER power (less log-like, more sqrt-like) than
everywhere else, and Region 5 sits at the opposite (more aggressive-
than-log) extreme, matching the EDA's reported per-location Box-Cox
range (-0.40 Region 5 to 0.29 Region 9) closely (this grid-search MLE
is coarser but lands within 0.06-0.10 of both reported extremes).

BUT: plugging all 11 fitted powers directly into the unshifted power
family (`w -> w^p`) is a disaster (per_location_boxcox: mean_wis=0.765,
more than DOUBLE the fourthroot baseline). Cause: `w^p` for p < 0
diverges as w -> 0, and most locations have near-zero wILI weeks every
summer trough; a location with a negative fitted power (US National,
Region 3/4/5/6) blows up its own modelling-scale values there, wrecking
the AR(6) fit and simulated forecast distribution for that location
(compare per-location mean WIS: Region 6 goes from 0.50 under `log` to
1.88 under naive per-location powers; Region 3/4/5/US National show the
same 3-6x blow-up). This is exactly why `src/core.jl`'s `TRANSFORMS`
implements `log` as its own branch (`log(max(w, EPS))`) rather than as
the p -> 0 limit of the unshifted power family: `log` is the
numerically stable choice whenever the fitted power is at or below 0,
which is most locations here.

Region 9's own fitted power (0.24) IS positive and numerically safe, so
a targeted override -- `log` everywhere except Region 9 -- was tested
two ways:

  log_r9_boxcox (Region 9 -> p=0.24 fitted): mean_wis=0.3534 (vs
  0.3537 plain log); Region 9's own per-task mean WIS improves from
  0.4559 (plain log) to 0.4522.
  log_r9_sqrt (Region 9 -> sqrt, p=0.5, the team's "Poisson-like"
  heuristic): mean_wis=0.3529 (best candidate overall); Region 9's own
  mean WIS improves further to 0.4470.

Both directions of the override help, `sqrt` slightly more than the
fitted p=0.24 -- consistent with Region 9's Taylor's-power-law slope
(lambda=0.91, docs/eda/07-region9-deepdive.md) implying an even higher
variance-stabilising power (p = 1 - lambda/2 ~ 0.55, almost exactly
sqrt) than its own direct Box-Cox MLE (0.24) suggests. The improvement
is real (consistent across both mixes, and both mean AND sd_wis move
in the same direction) but small in absolute terms: log -> log_r9_sqrt
is a further ~0.2% mean-WIS gain on top of log's already-large ~4% gain
over fourthroot. Every other location is left on plain `log`
unchanged (their per-location mean WIS is identical to plain `log`'s
column in the sweep table, by construction).
