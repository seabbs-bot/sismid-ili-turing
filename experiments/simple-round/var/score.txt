Multi-location AR/VAR sweep, simple-model wide round
=====================================================

Family: submissions/nfidd-ar6/generate_forecasts.jl (plain per-location
AR(p), OLS on fourthroot-transformed vintage series, 1000 simulated
Gaussian-innovation paths). Own AR(6) is kept unchanged throughout;
what varies is whether/how OTHER locations' recent values are added as
extra predictors, and how heavily any cross-location coefficients are
regularised. All candidates simulate forward JOINTLY across all 11
locations (h=1..4): because the cross-location predictors are lagged
(never lag 0), a later horizon's simulated value for one location
genuinely depends on every location's simulated value at the previous
horizon.

Scored on VALIDATION SEASONS ONLY (1, 2 = 2015/16, 2016/17), against
target-data/oracle-output.csv in the local hub clone
(~/code/external/sismid-ili-forecasting-sandbox). WIS is computed
in-line (same formula as src/scoring.jl's `wis`) to avoid the
ScoringRules dependency and stay on the light CSV/DataFrames/
Statistics/LinearAlgebra stack. Test seasons (3-5) were never
generated, scored, or looked at during tuning. Produced by sweep.jl in
this directory; total sweep runtime 61.7s (13 candidates x 59 origins
x 11 locations, all in-process).

Reference points from submissions/seabbs_bot-ar6bf/README.md:
nfidd-ar6 = 0.368 (sd 0.471), seabbs_bot-ar6bf = 0.359 (sd 0.452).

Candidate definitions
----------------------

- ar6_baseline: plain per-location AR(6), no cross terms (sanity-check
  replicate of nfidd-ar6; 0.3684 here vs 0.368 reported there, the gap
  is Monte-Carlo path-simulation noise from a different RNG stream).
- var1_*: own AR(6) + ALL 10 other locations' lag-1 value, ridge
  penalty lambda applied ONLY to the 10 cross-location coefficients
  (own AR(6) lags and the intercept are never penalised). lambda in
  {0 (= var1_ols), 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 3, 5}.
- var2_*: as var1, plus each other location's lag-2 value too (a full
  VAR(2)), same ridge treatment.
- ar_nat_ols: own AR(6) + `US National`'s lag-1 value only (a single,
  reduced-rank common-factor predictor), OLS (no ridge -- only one
  extra, low-risk parameter).
- ar_neighbor_ols: own AR(6) + the single most-correlated OTHER
  location's lag-1 value, computed from the training window itself
  (not hand-coded HHS-region geography) so it is refit per split, OLS.
- ar_nat_neighbor_ols: both of the above combined, OLS.

Chosen: var1_ridge1.5 (ridge-penalised VAR(1) on top of AR(6)),
mean WIS 0.3383
  - beats nfidd-ar6 by ~8.1% (0.368 -> 0.3383)
  - beats seabbs_bot-ar6bf by ~5.8% (0.359 -> 0.3383)

Does cross-location information help? Yes, but only once regularised.
------------------------------------------------------------------------

- The unpenalised dense VAR(1) (var1_ols, 11 locations x 1 lag = 10
  extra predictors per location on top of the own AR(6)) scores WORSE
  than the plain AR(6) baseline (0.4214 vs 0.3684): with
  window_weeks=104 (~98 usable rows per split after the AR(6) lag cut)
  and highly collinear predictors (every location's raw level co-moves
  strongly, docs/eda/04-cross-location.md's level-correlation mean
  0.76), 10 extra unregularised coefficients overfit badly. This is
  exactly the risk the EDA flagged ("a full dense VAR(1)/VAR(2) over
  11 locations overfits, so REGULARISE").
- Ridge-penalising just the cross-location block fixes this
  completely and then helps: mean WIS falls from 0.3684 (no cross
  info) to 0.3383 (ridge=1.5) as lambda is dialled in from "none" (var1_ols,
  badly overfit) through a broad, FLAT optimum around lambda=1-2
  (0.3383-0.3389, well within Monte-Carlo noise of each other) up to
  lambda=5 (0.3446, drifting back towards the unpooled baseline as the
  cross-location coefficients are shrunk towards 0).
- VAR(2) (adding lag-2 cross terms) never beats the best VAR(1) variant
  (var2_ridge5's 0.3391 vs var1_ridge1.5's 0.3383, and var2_ridge50's
  0.3565 is worse than var1_ridge3's 0.3408 at a comparable
  regularisation strength) -- consistent with
  docs/eda/04-cross-location.md's lead-lag finding that every region's
  cross-correlation with the national series peaks at lag 0, not
  lag >= 1: there is no genuine extra lagged information at lag 2 to
  recover, so its only effect is to add more parameters to regularise
  away.
- The lighter "own AR + a couple of extra predictors" family
  (ar_nat_ols, ar_neighbor_ols, ar_nat_neighbor_ols: 0.3518-0.3527)
  does help over the plain baseline (a smaller, ~4% gain), but far
  less than the full ridge-VAR(1) (~8%): a single national or
  nearest-neighbour lag-1 predictor captures only part of the
  cross-location signal that the full (regularised) 10-predictor
  cross-location block captures.
- Net conclusion: the EDA's own prediction holds exactly -- cross-
  location coupling in this data is real and useful for forecasting,
  but it must be heavily shrunk (a handful of extra predictors is not
  enough; a dense VAR needs a ridge penalty of a similar order of
  magnitude to the own-AR fit itself) to avoid overfitting the
  ~100-observation training window.
